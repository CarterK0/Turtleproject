Kemp's Ridley Sea Turtle

(Lepidochelys kempii)

Status: Federally Endangered (HIGHEST CONSERVATION CONCERN in Alabama)

Slide 1: General Species Profile

  • Size: Smallest sea turtle species, typically 24-28 inches (60-70 cm) in shell length.
  • Weight: Adults weigh between 70 to 100 pounds (32-45 kg).
  • Appearance: Oval, olive-gray carapace (top shell) and a white/pale yellow plastron (bottom shell). Hatchlings are solid black.
  • Diet: Primarily benthic feeders, meaning they eat food found on the bottom, mainly **blue crabs**, but also shrimp, clams, and fish.
  • Lifespan: Estimated to be around 30 to 50 years.

Slide 2: Habitat and Alabama Presence

Primary Habitat

Kemp's ridleys primarily inhabit the shallow, nearshore coastal waters (neritic zone) of the Gulf of Mexico. They prefer areas with muddy or sandy bottoms where their crab prey is abundant. Juveniles often drift in the open ocean, hiding and feeding in floating **Sargassum seaweed** mats for the first 1-2 years of life.

Alabama Distribution (Critically Important Area)

  • The species is an **occasional visitor** to Alabama's coastal and bay waters (e.g., Mobile Bay and Gulf Shores beaches).
  • Alabama's coastal waters are historically significant as crucial **foraging areas** (feeding grounds) for juvenile Kemp's ridleys, especially when blue crabs are plentiful.
  • Nesting in Alabama: While 95% of nesting occurs in Mexico, occasional, individual nests have been documented on Alabama beaches (at least two historically documented).
  • The presence of Kemp's ridleys in the northern Gulf exposes them to threats like incidental capture in shrimp nets, making their protection in Alabama waters vital.

Slide 3: Unique Nesting Habits

The "Arribada"

  • Kemp's ridleys are famous for their synchronized mass nesting events called **arribadas** (Spanish for "arrival").
  • Large groups of females gather offshore and come ashore simultaneously to nest over a few days. This behavior is believed to overwhelm predators.
  • Unique Feature: They are the **only sea turtle species that routinely nests during the daytime.**
  • Nesting Season: Primarily April through July.
  • Clutch Size: Females lay an average of 100 eggs per nest and may lay 2-3 clutches per season.

Slide 4: Movement and Track Analysis

Locomotion for Tracking Model

Marine Movement: Kemp's ridleys are highly migratory, using their front flippers for powerful propulsion while swimming great distances between foraging and nesting grounds.

Terrestrial Movement (Nesting Track Pattern)

When adult females emerge onto the beach to nest, they pull their bodies forward using their powerful front flippers. The pattern they leave in the sand is crucial for species identification and nest monitoring.

AI Tracking Model Input: Track Characteristics

  • Track Width: Approximately 2 feet (60 cm) wide.
  • Flipper Motion: **Alternating Gait** (one front flipper pushes, then the other).
  • Track Appearance: The track marks left by the flippers are staggered, and the marks often resemble a **comma shape** in the sand.
  • Center Mark: A narrow, relatively continuous tail drag mark is usually visible down the center of the track.
