2. Species Overview & Status

Scientific Name: Caretta caretta

Description: Named for its large head and powerful jaws, used to crush hard-shelled prey like conchs, clams, and sea urchins. Their shells (carapace) are reddish-brown.

Adult Size: 170 to 500 lbs, with a carapace length up to 45 inches.

Lifespan: 30 to 50+ years, reaching sexual maturity between 32 and 35 years.

Conservation Status

Global: Listed as Vulnerable (IUCN).

U.S. Status: Federally Threatened.

Alabama Focus: Loggerheads in Alabama belong to the Northern Gulf of Mexico distinct population segment, which is one of the smallest recovery units, making local conservation efforts critical.

3. Habitat & Nesting in Alabama

Alabama Distribution & Habitat

General Habitat: Loggerheads are adaptable, found both hundreds of miles offshore (pelagic) and in shallow coastal areas like bays, lagoons, and salt marshes (neritic).

Found in Alabama: They are the most common nesting sea turtle species in Alabama.

  • Counties: Baldwin and Mobile.
  • Beaches: All Gulf coast beaches, from Dauphin Island's west end to Alabama Point, with the majority of nesting activity concentrated between Fort Morgan and Gulf Shores.

Nesting Habits

  • Nesting Season: May 1st to October 31st (Peak: June-July).
  • Process: Females nest at night, returning to their natal beaches. They prefer high-energy, coarse-grained beaches.
  • Clutch Size & Frequency: They lay 1 to 7 nests per season (average ~4), with an interval of about 14 days between each nesting event. Each nest contains approximately 110-126 eggs.
  • Hatching: Eggs incubate for 42 to 75 days. Hatchlings emerge mostly at night and use light and shadow cues to navigate to the sea.

4. Locomotion & AI Tracking Data

Movement Style

  • Aquatic Movement (Adults): Primarily use a "submarine flight" or flapping motion, moving their large front flippers up and down simultaneously like wings. This is highly efficient for long migrations.
  • Terrestrial Movement (Nesting/Hatching): Use a crawling or rowing motion. Diagonal limbs (front-left and rear-right, then front-right and rear-left) move together, pushing the heavy body forward.

Track Description for AI Model

The key identifier for Loggerhead tracks is the alternating flipper marks (similar to human walking, not synchronized).

  • Track Width: Typically 3.5 to 4 feet wide.
  • Flipper Pattern: Shows an alternating, asymmetrical pattern created by the front flippers.
  • Body Drag: A well-defined, generally straight central trough or drag mark from the plastron (bottom shell) is often present, but sometimes subtle in large females.

5. Resources for Further AI Research

Official Alabama Conservation

  • Alabama Sea Turtle Conservation Program: A crucial local source for nest data, patrols, and specific research on Alabama nesting success. (Outdoor Alabama - Reproductive Study)
  • NOAA Fisheries: Provides detailed scientific data on population status, migration, and protective measures (TEDs). (NOAA Loggerhead Profile)

Videos & Visual Data (AI Input)

  • Tracking Turtle Migrations: Satellite tag data provides excellent datasets on migration paths, speed, and dive profiles (key features for a comprehensive AI model).
